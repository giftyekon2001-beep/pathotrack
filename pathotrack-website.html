Health Awareness & Prevention

Learn how to protect yourself and your community

🦠

Typhoid Prevention

What is it? Typhoid is a serious bacterial infection spread through contaminated water and food. It causes high fever, abdominal pain, and weakness.

Prevention: Drink clean water, practice good hygiene, ensure proper food preparation, and get vaccinated if traveling to high-risk areas.

🧼

Hygiene Practices

Key Habits: Wash hands frequently with soap and water, especially before eating and after using the toilet. Keep your living space clean and well-ventilated.

Important: Cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing, and dispose of tissues properly to prevent disease transmission.

🍎

Safe Food Handling

Dos: Cook food thoroughly, keep raw and cooked foods separate, refrigerate food promptly, and wash fruits and vegetables before consumption.

Avoid: Eating street food from unhygienic sources, consuming expired items, or food prepared in unsanitary conditions.

💊

Proper Antibiotic Use

Best Practice: Only take antibiotics when prescribed by a doctor. Never share medications or use antibiotics for viral infections like flu or colds.

Important: Complete the full course of antibiotics even if you feel better. Misuse leads to antibiotic resistance and treatment failures.

💧

Clean Water Access

Safe Water: Drink water from trusted sources. Boil water if unsure about its safety. Use water filters or purification tablets when necessary.

Storage: Keep water in clean, covered containers. Never drink from stagnant water sources or unverified wells.

🛡️

When to Seek Help

Visit a Doctor If: You have persistent fever (>3 days), severe abdominal pain, blood in stool, difficulty breathing, or symptoms that worsen rapidly.

Emergency: Seek immediate medical attention for confusion, loss of consciousness, or severe dehydration.
